Seminar Description

Prerequisite: Linear Algebra, Abstract Algebra (including groups, rings, modules), Basic Number Theory, and basic familiarity with complexity theory and cryptography (e.g., one-way functions, public-key encryption).

This seminar offers a rigorous introduction to lattice-based cryptography, one of the most promising directions in post-quantum cryptography. Topics include worst-case to average-case reductions, the Learning With Errors (LWE) problem, ring/ideal variants, trapdoors, digital signatures, identity-based and functional encryption, as well as advanced constructions such as fully homomorphic encryption and zero-knowledge proofs.

Emphasis will be placed on both theoretical foundations and practical relevance. The course will follow a mix of lecture presentations and student-led paper discussions.

By the end of the seminar, students should be equipped to read cutting-edge research papers and conduct independent work in lattice-based cryptography or related fields in post-quantum cryptography.
